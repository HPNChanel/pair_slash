# PairSlash Anti-goals

## PairSlash does not become

- a new agent framework
- an autonomous coding platform
- a generic wiki or note-taking system
- a broad workflow automation suite for every use case
- a third-runtime compatibility layer
- a feature bucket justified by novelty instead of pain

## Why those directions are dangerous

- They dilute the trust-layer thesis.
- They pull the product toward breadth before proving a wedge.
- They turn installability into fake evidence of demand.
- They invite feature work that does not reduce rework, context loss, or trust gaps.
- They make it easier to market the product and harder to make it habit-forming.

## Signs of product drift

- README language starts centering "platform" or "framework" instead of trust.
- New ideas cannot be mapped to repo understanding, safe repeated workflows, or disciplined memory.
- Roadmap items sound exciting but do not reduce almost-right rework or project-context loss.
- Benchmarks reward breadth or demo polish more than trust and repeat use.
- Setup work grows faster than the proof that users will come back next week.
